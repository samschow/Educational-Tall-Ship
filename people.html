Staff

Alan Olson

Executive Director


Alan heads the project and brings the vision to the organization. Alan’s 50 years of maritime experience, in construction, engineering, restoration and sailing, makes him uniquely suited to run such a project. In 1961, Alan built a 40 ft catamaran in Minnesota and sailed down the Mississippito the Gulf of Mexico and the Caribbean. His work in boat yards in Florida included being lead carpenter on the building of a 85 foot catamaran and solidified his interest and passion for everything nautical.In 1971 he began construction of Stone Witch, a 70’ brigantine . From 1977 on,he sailed Stone Witch over 40,000 miles, operating educational sailing programs for youth and adults along with public charters on San Francisco Bay. Stone Witch was the flag ship for Greenpeace and involved in many environmental missions along the California Coast. The majority of that time Stone Witch operated without an engine, operating in a traditional manner requiring great skill and leadership.In 1984 Alan founded the non-profit organization Call of the Sea. Call of the Sea’s mission is to provide a challenging, hands-on, educational experience aboard a traditional sailing vessel. In 2005, Call of the Sea’s 82’ schooner Seaward was acquired and has served over 15,000 students while sailing over 40,000 miles on day and overnight educational youth programs.In the 1980’s Alan restored a 1929, 54ft wooden staysail schooner to use in youth educational programs with Call of the Sea and on educational expeditions with adults that included seven trips to Mexico and a 15,000 mile Pacific Rim voyage that included China.

Kim Kouri

Director of Development


Kim brings fundraising and marketing expertise to the team, having just spent her last seven years as the Director of Institutional Advancement at Marin Horizon School. Prior to that, she was a Vice President in a marketing firm, specializing in non-profits. Along with experience, Kim brings energy and enthusiasm to the project and a passion for programs that foster education through outdoor experiences and hands-on learning. Kim will be taking sailing lessons this summer. She has a BA in Political Science and Women's Studies from Loyola Marymount University.

Sam Schow

Educational Program Coordinator


Sam comes to the project as an experienced outdoor educator, having worked for six years as a backpacking and kayaking guide in the Bay Area and Yosemite, a naturalist in the coastal wilderness of Northern California, and a surf instructor. Before joining the team at ETS, Sam worked as a substitute teacher and outdoor education specialist at Marin Horizon School in Mill Valley, California. Sam began his career as a sailor at the age of 12 in Sausalito Yacht Club’s Youth Sailing summer program. Since then, he has sailed for recreation in the Bay and on the sailing club team at Skidmore College in NY. Sam holds a BA in music from Skidmore College.

Marc Bauer

Construction Superintendent


Marc brings the direct know-how of how to construct a tall ship. His experience as the project superintendent during the construction of the Virginia by Tri-Coastal Marine uniquely qualifies him to run the construction of the Educational Tall Ship. The Virginia is a traditional tall ship with a similar size and configuration as the Educational Tall Ship and the skills involved in her construction will translate well to our project. In addition to his obvious qualifications for this project, Marc also brings a background of marine design and restoration projects. Marc has a Bachelors degree from Virginia Commonwealth University and a degree in design from The Landing School.
